Clouds and Latitude

When analyzing the cloud coverage between both the northern and southern latitudes, it is difficult to correlate any trend statistically which is confirmed when applying a linear regression model. One thing to note, is that a pocket of zero cloud coverage can be commonly found at 23.5 N & S. This can be attributed to convection trend cells located from 0° - 23.5°N, 23.5°N -90°N and the same latitudinal coordinates for the southern hemisphere. The same can be assumed for the southern hemisphere however as we reach 90°S, there is a significant decrease in landmass and therefore ability to gather datapoints.
